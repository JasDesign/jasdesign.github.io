JASMIN SONG  

Design is my biggest hobby that I never get bored. I am always excited when I suddenly generate a new idea from nowhere or get inspired by different things. I enjoy the process of seeking solutions, thinking in depth and exploring insights and opportunities. My experience in Industrial design ranging from consumer product, furniture, hardware to mobility. I am skilled in the design process from concept to lunch, and actively absorbing new knowledge and techniques to enrich my ability.

 

Starts at a sparkle, design is the process of merging inspiration with thoughts, refining details with knowledge, and compromise aesthetics with function, to make it reach as close as to perfect. But lots of good selling products are far from the balance point in between aesthetics and functionality. The design was given up in the middle. It is normal that new challenges continuously occur during optimization. Solving problems makes the design more sophisticated. Design is never end.

 

Good design brings refreshing perspective, appealing look and authentic function to users. There are too many similar product designs only follow the trend without rethinking the problem and exploring new opportunities, that fail to generate sustainable profit during the competition. Design aims to provide unique experience and smarter solution, not to add more goods on the market.

 

My design philosophy:

 

• Think out of the box

Try to think out of the box can bring a fresh eye to the problem and discover new opportunities. For instance, as to design a chair, forget about the chair, try to think of designing something can sit; Behavior observation also provides clues of hidden problems in familiar situation; Imagining a futuristic life style breaks the mindset and generates new possibilities.

Innovative and thoughtful solution makes a difference and would revolutionary change the rule.

 

• Sensory design

I have an experience in volunteering for blind people that made me rethink the ways to perceive the world and inspired my design. As technology pushes us to be ever more focused towards the visual sense, we are losing track of our other senses, and spending less and less time experiencing the world firsthand.

A consumer who uses a product receives continuous feedback about the product’s performance through the various sensory modalities in regard of product safety, ease of use, and product enjoyment. What if we designed for all of our senses? Sensory design considers the nature of human beings to arrive at integrated designs in which all senses are activated: sight, hear¬ing, taste, touch, and smell. Those senses fuel people’s emotions, wielding the power to persuade, relax and heal. Designers who intentionally try to create specific experiences for people, such as delight, trust or the feeling of being cared for, are more likely to succeed if they are aware of the messages conveyed by the different sensory channels and of their contribution to the overall experience.

“It’s important for designers to raise their perception of sensory data and not just focus exclusively on the visual aesthetic. By recall¬ing their own sensory experiences in their designs, designers can create more humane built products for people.”

- Joy Monice, Sensory Design

 

Reference: 1. Joy Monice, Sensory Deisgn, 2004

2. NeuroSci, Multisensory Product Design, 2014

3. Hendrik N. J. Schifferstein, Multi-sensory design, 2011
