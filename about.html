About Me

Design is my biggest hobby that I never get bored of. I am always excited when I suddenly generate a new idea from nowhere or get inspired by different things. I enjoy the process of seeking solutions, thinking in depth, and exploring insights and opportunities. My experience in industrial design ranges from consumer products, furniture, and hardware to mobility. I am skilled in all the stages of the design process from its conceptualization to its launch, and I am always actively absorbing new knowledges and techniques to further enrich my ability.

Starting at a sparkle, design is the process of merging inspiration with thoughts, refining details with knowledge, and compromising aesthetics with function to make it reach as close to perfect as possible. Lots of good selling products are far from the balance point between aesthetics and functionality. The design was given up in the middle. It is normal that new challenges continuously occur during optimization. Solving problems makes the design more sophisticated. Design never ends.

Good design brings a refreshing perspective, an appealing look, and an authentic function to users. There are too many similar product designs that only follow the trend without rethinking the problem and exploring new opportunities, and that fail to generate sustainable profit during the competition. Design aims to provide a unique experience and a smarter solution instead of adding more goods to the market.

My Design Philosophy:

• Think out of the box

Trying to think out of the box can bring a fresh eye to the problem and help one discover new opportunities. For instance, when it comes to designing a chair, forget about the chair and try to think of designing something that can be sat on; behavior observation also provides clues of hidden problems in familiar situation; imagining a futuristic life style breaks the mindset and generates new possibilities. Innovative and thoughtful solutions make a difference and being revolutionary would change the rule.

• Apply sensory design with emotional connection

I have an experience in volunteering for blind people to help them do training, gardening and daily activities that made me rethink the ways of perceiving the world and inspired my design. As technology pushes us to be ever more focused towards the visual sense, we are losing track of our other senses, and spending less and less time experiencing the world firsthand.

A consumer who uses a product receives continuous feedback about the product’s performance through the various sensory modalities regarding product safety, ease of use, and product enjoyment.1 What if we designed for all our senses? Sensory design considers the nature of human beings to arrive at integrated designs in which all senses are activated: sight, hearing, taste, touch, and smell. Those senses fuel people’s emotions, wielding the power to persuade, relax, and heal.2 Designers who intentionally try to create specific experiences for people, such as delight, trust, or the feeling of being cared for, are more likely to succeed if they are aware of the messages conveyed by the different sensory channels and their contribution to the overall experience.3

“It’s important for designers to raise their perception of sensory data and not just focus exclusively on the visual aesthetic. By recalling their own sensory experiences in their designs, designers can create more humane built products for people.”

– Joy Monice, Sensory Design

• Fuse culture, art, nature, fashion, technology, and psychology into design.

A design, like a person, has a unique character and its own story based on the growth environment influenced by culture, art, nature, fashion, technology, and psychology that makes it stand out and creates meaningful connections with people.

• Strive for the balance between aesthetics and functionality.

Overcoming the challenge of optimization makes the design stronger.

I want to make a difference.

I want to shape the future for people.

Design has become the grounding force in my life that allows me to compose, to break through, and to seize control of my destiny.
