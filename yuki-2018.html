Features

- Snowy mountain shaped design.

- Bubble massage spa experience.

- Simplistic control system.

- L 64”, W 64”, H 25”.

- 18.5” soaking depth.

- Accommodates up to 2 bathers.

- Material: Acrylic.

One of best things to do in Japan is enjoying Rotenburo (outdoor hot spring) with a spectacular snowy scenery in winter. Yuki spa bathtub brings the blissful experience home.

Yuki means "snow" in Japanese. Inspired by the beauty of snow-tipped Mt. Fuji, Yuki spa tub combines white and translucent acrylic to create a scenery of snow and ice with a mountain silhouette and delivers a sensation of purity and peace. The elements of water, snow, ice and steam represent “One is All; All is One” in Eastern philosophy of harmony. Relaxing in the Yuki spa tub, you will feel as if you are soaking in a snow-covered hot spring.

Featuring a unique square design, Yuki spa bathtub explores the connection between aesthetic harmony and ergonomic usage. It lets you lay on any side of the tub to enjoy deep, immersive bathing. Surrounding air jets release thousands of bubbles to massage your body at the level of intensity you prefer by simply pressing a button. Unlike other spa tub's incongruous and bulky control panels, the control button thoughtfully designed with low-key elegance and ease of use. The coated acrylic material has the attractive finish of smooth stone but feels warm to the touch and is easy to clean.

Yuki spa tub blends sensory design with contemporary flair to transform any bathroom into an oasis of relaxation.

Yuki Spa Tub

The button on Yuki spa tub activates the 60 high-powered air jets surrounding the interior of the spa for a soothing bubble massage at the level of intensity you prefer. The 360° designed air jets placed on the lower perimeter of the bath stimulates the release of muscle tension and helps center the mind.

How to use

- Press and hold for 3 sec to activate/deactivate the air jets.

- Press to switch the levels of bubble intensity, from light, medium to high volume.

- The system remembers your last setting when turning on.
